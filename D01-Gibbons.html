System Design Principles

CMPT 330 111 - Professor Tokash

Principle Categories:

Key Principles:

Visibility

Consistency

Feedback

Constraints

Affordances

Accessibility

General Usability:

No manual should be necessary

Provide enough options, but not too many

Options should be relevant and readily available

Provide feedback for actions

Use easily recognizable affordances and signifiers

Create a consistent, understandable conceptional model

Present information neatly, concisely, and in chunks

Do not overwhelm users with information

Accessibility and Cultural Sensitivity:

Show sensitivity to your audience's needs and views

Follow the target audience's social norms

Use colors and layouts that meet user expectations

Design with screen readers and text scaling in mind

Understand cognition's impacts on usability

Adjust for multiple learning styles

Use multiple forms of indicators as needed

Provide easily accessible documentation and help pages

User Experience:

Design with multitasking in mind

Relate a system's concepts together

Systems should not be intrusive, but still be ever-ready

Do not assume what users need; investigate first

Use metadata to help users organize and remember things

Let users prioritize the features they need

Appeal to the user's senses

Ethics and Emotions:

Use persuasion... with care!

Show empathy

Understand the user's relationship to the system

Only collect data which is necessary

Provide transparency

Gather and respond to user feedback
